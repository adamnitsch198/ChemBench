Abstract
On the identification of residual solvent peaks in NMR spectra

Residual solvent signals are among the most frequently encountered artifacts in routine NMR spectroscopy. Accurate identification of these peaks is essential for characterisation of synthetic products and assessment of sample purity. ChemBench provides a systematically organised reference for 1H and 13C chemical shifts of common laboratory solvents, impurities, stabilisers, and degradation products across twelve deuterated solvent systems.

Peak data is sourced from peer-reviewed literature and presented with a confidence-scored identification engine, supporting both single- and multi-peak queries. The drying agent module complements the NMR reference by providing compatibility-aware recommendations for solvent desiccation.

Capabilities

01
Multi-peak identification
Enter multiple chemical shifts simultaneously. The engine cross-references all entries to identify compounds matching the full peak pattern.
02
Confidence scoring
Each candidate receives a percentage confidence score derived from ppm proximity, multiplicity agreement, and peak set coverage.
03
Spectrum visualisation
Interactive Lorentzian spectrum rendering with correct multiplicity patterns - singlets through septets - with hover identification.
04
Stabiliser database
BHT, amylene, ethanol, propylene oxide, MEHQ, triethylamine, and additional stabilisers - each tagged to their parent solvent.
05
Drying agent compatibility
Functional group-aware drying agent recommendations with ranked output, hazard assessment, and dosage and procedural guidance.
06
Peer-reviewed data
All NMR reference data is drawn from Fulmer et al. 2010 and Whiteker et al. 2016 - the established standards in the field.
